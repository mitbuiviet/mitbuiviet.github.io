Deutsch made-soft for German

    Why made soft? German language (of German people) is, as a cliché, rumored to sound harsh and überstrong compared to other some european peers like Français or Italiano.my learning and tips

    I started off learning German my second year in Japan. It was highly enjoyable to learn German in Japan with help solely from the Internet. Of course, I had language partners vie Italki but my German improves mostly from active learning through videos on YouTube.

  My recommendation is always Easy German. My general tip is: natural and repeated (sometimes overboard) immersion in the target language.

  At the moment, I can communicate well enough in daily situations. I can read even academic texts in assisted context.

  German is so important to me since it marks my first self-study success when it comes to foreign language.

日本語 the angelic Japanese language

    My road to Japanese commences out of painful irrationality. Later on, it became the required, instead of a choice.uhhh... angelic?

    Yes, it is angelic. I am talking about how beautiful it sounds, and the way people connect it to plenty of lovely cheesy things like Sakura, anime, cute kitty. Moreover, it is angelic in the way that its grammar is godly difficult plus thousand of Kanjis which are pronounced in irregular manners. And unfortunately, Kanji learning is a must.

   Well I would admit I had my advantage in Kanji as a Vietnamese. My understanding of Japanese grew pretty quickly after I learned by heart around 2000 Kanjis' meaning and reading.

   After that was just literally cramming.I read a lot of grammar books and did a huge amount of exercises. I also immersed myself in Japanese with a TV, real books. I love reading easily explained books by Ikegami Akira, also Marie Kondo wrote quite plain but interesting books about minimalism.

    Finally, pretty much like English, I am generally fluent in Japanese. I take most of the classes in Japanese and it is principal communicating language I use in university.
